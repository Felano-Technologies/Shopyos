1. What is Shopyos?

Shopyos is a mobile marketplace operating in Ghana where buyers can purchase goods from registered sellers and have them delivered — either locally within the same region or through a parcel hub network for buyers and sellers in different regions.

The platform also includes features such as price negotiation (bargaining), time-limited discounts (flash sales), seller promotional content (snaps), and a seller advertising system. Shopyos earns revenue through commissions on seller payouts, delivery fees, and seller advertising spend.

3. Account Registration & User Agreement

Before a user can create an account, they must actively tick two checkboxes to confirm they have read and agreed to:

  • Terms of Service — the rules governing acceptable use of the platform, account security, and the grounds for suspension or termination.
  • Privacy Policy — how Shopyos collects, stores, and uses personal data, and the user's data rights under the Ghana Data Protection Act, 2012 (Act 843).

If either box is left unchecked, registration is blocked. When a user registers successfully, the date and time of their consent, along with their device type and IP address, is permanently recorded in the platform's consent database.

Legal Significance No account can be created without this recorded consent. The record is permanent and cannot be altered or deleted.

4. Buying on Shopyos

Checkout Agreement

Before any payment is processed, the buyer is shown the Refund & Cancellation Policy in a pop-up window. They must tick a checkbox and press "Agree" before the platform allows the payment to proceed. The system also blocks the order on the server side if this agreement was not formally recorded — so there is no way to pay without having consented.

What Buyers Agree to at Checkout

  • Delivery fees are non-refundable once the order has been paid.
  • The buyer protection fee (if applicable) is also non-refundable.
  • Cancellations are only possible within 5 minutes of placing an order, provided it has not yet been processed.
  • After a rider picks up the order, no cancellation or refund is available.

5. Refunds & Returns

When a Buyer Can Request a Return

Buyers may apply for a return within the return window (currently 7 days from delivery, adjustable by admin). Returns go through a three-step approval process: seller review → optional admin override.

Refund Rules (Fixed by Platform Logic)

SituationRefund Outcome
Order cancelled before paymentFull refund
Order cancelled after payment, before rider picks upProduct price refunded; delivery fee not refunded
Order cancelled after rider picks upNo refund
Return approved (within return window)Product price refunded; delivery fee deducted
Return deniedNo refund

Consent Before Return Submission

Before a buyer can submit a return request, the platform shows the refund policy again and requires the buyer to tick a checkbox confirming they understand that the delivery fee is non-refundable. The server also enforces this — a return request cannot be submitted without the recorded agreement.

Exact Refund Amount Shown Before Submission

The return screen shows the buyer the exact amount they will receive back before they complete the request, so there can be no dispute about the amount being unexpected.

6. Delivery & Shipping

Local Delivery

When a buyer and seller are in the same region, a local driver is assigned to pick up and deliver the order. The delivery fee is set by the platform and displayed to the buyer at checkout.

Inter-Regional Delivery (Hub Network)

When a buyer and seller are in different regions, the parcel is routed through a network of dispatch hubs. Every time the parcel moves between hubs or changes status, this is recorded in a permanent log that includes who updated it and when — creating a full chain of custody.

Last-Mile Home Delivery

Once a parcel arrives at the buyer's nearest hub, the buyer may optionally choose to have it delivered to their home address (last-mile delivery). Before this can be confirmed, the buyer must read and agree to the Inter-Regional Shipping Terms. These terms explain:

  • Transit time estimates are not guarantees.
  • If home delivery is not requested, the buyer must collect from the hub.
  • Delivery costs for inter-regional orders are not refunded on return.

The last-mile fee is shown to the buyer before they confirm, and the consent is recorded permanently.

7. Price Bargaining

Buyers can negotiate a lower price with sellers on certain products. Before submitting an offer, the buyer must read and agree to the Bargaining Terms & Conditions, which state:

  • Once a seller accepts an offer, it is a binding agreement.
  • The buyer must complete checkout within a set time window (typically 1 hour). If they do not, the deal expires and the full price applies.
  • Buyers can only see their own offers — no buyer ever sees what price another buyer offered or what the seller's counter-price is.
  • The original listed price on the product page does not change; the agreed discount is applied only at checkout for that specific buyer.

The consent is recorded against the specific product, so in any dispute the platform can show exactly when the buyer agreed to these terms for that transaction.

8. Flash Sales

Sellers can apply to sell products at a reduced price during a specific time slot (a "flash sale"). The process is as follows:

  1. Seller submits a product and price for a flash sale slot.
  2. Before submitting, the seller must read and agree to the Flash Sale Agreement — covering rules about minimum stock levels, pricing locks, and slot compliance.
  3. The submission goes to admin for review. It is either approved or rejected; no product appears in a flash sale without admin sign-off.
  4. Once approved, stock is reserved automatically and atomically (the system prevents overselling at the database level).
  5. All buyers see the same flash sale feed — there is no preferential access or personalised pricing.
Open Issue Legal counsel should confirm whether flash sale "no-return" clauses are compliant with Ghana's Consumer Protection Agency Act, 2012 (Act 843). See Section 17 for the full list of open items.

9. Selling on Shopyos

Business Registration

To open a store, a seller must submit their business details (name, category, and optional documents such as a business certificate or proof of bank account) and agree to the Seller Commission Agreement. This agreement states that Shopyos automatically deducts a platform commission from each completed order before the seller is paid out.

This agreement must be accepted before the store is created. The consent is permanently recorded.

Commission Rates

Commission rates are set by Shopyos administrators and can be adjusted. Every time an admin changes a commission rate, the old rate, new rate, the admin who made the change, the reason given, and the date are recorded in a permanent audit log. This ensures full accountability for any pricing decisions that could be disputed.

10. Seller Payouts

When a seller requests a payout of their earned balance, they must first agree to the Payout Guidelines. These terms cover:

  • Payouts are only made to verified bank accounts on file.
  • Processing times are subject to bank transfer schedules and Shopyos review periods.
  • Admin approval is required before any payout is disbursed.

The seller's consent to these guidelines is recorded at the time of each payout request.

11. Drivers & Delivery Partners

Drivers who register on the platform agree to the Driver Earnings & Splits terms, which explain that drivers earn a percentage share of delivery fees from completed orders. The exact percentage is set by platform administrators.

This consent is recorded at the time of driver account creation.

Open Issue The question of who bears liability for goods damaged or lost during driver transit is not yet covered in the driver agreement. Legal counsel should advise on this.

12. Reviews & Ratings

Buyers can leave star ratings and written reviews for products, stores, and drivers. Before submitting any review, the user must agree to the Review & Ratings Policy, which states:

  • Reviews must be genuine and based on the user's real experience.
  • Reviews must not be defamatory, offensive, or paid for.
  • Shopyos may remove reviews that violate community guidelines.
  • The user grants Shopyos the right to display their review on the platform.

This consent is enforced both on the app screen and at the server level — a review cannot be submitted without the recorded agreement.

13. Seller-Uploaded Content (Snaps)

"Snaps" are short photo or video posts that sellers publish to promote their products. Before a seller can publish a Snap, they must agree to the Seller Content & Media Policy, which states:

  • The seller confirms they own or have the right to use all images and videos they upload.
  • The content must not infringe any copyright or intellectual property rights.
  • The seller grants Shopyos a licence to display the content on the platform.
  • Misleading, offensive, or illegal content is prohibited.

This reduces the platform's exposure to intellectual property claims from third parties.

14. Advertising & Promotions

Sellers can pay to promote their products or place banner advertisements on the platform. Before creating any campaign, they must agree to the Advertising & Promotions Terms, which state:

  • All ad content must be truthful and compliant with Ghanaian advertising standards.
  • Campaign fees are non-refundable once the campaign is live.
  • Shopyos reserves the right to reject any campaign that does not meet platform standards.

Consent is required separately for promoted product campaigns and banner campaigns, and is recorded at the time of each submission.

15. Data, Privacy & Record-Keeping

What Data is Collected

  • Name, email address, and phone number at registration.
  • Device type and IP address at login and at every consent event.
  • Location data (used for delivery routing).
  • Order history, payment method tokens (not full card numbers), and chat messages.

How Data is Stored

All data is stored on a cloud database. Consent records, in particular, are stored in an append-only table — meaning no consent record can be modified or deleted once created. This is an intentional design decision to protect both the user and the platform in any dispute.

User Rights

Users are informed at registration (via the Privacy Policy) that they have the right to request access to, correction of, or deletion of their personal data in accordance with the Ghana Data Protection Act, 2012 (Act 843).

Open Issue Legal counsel should advise on the minimum data retention period for consent records. The current system retains records indefinitely. GDPR guidance suggests a minimum of 6 months; local law may differ.

Each policy has a version number (e.g. "1.0"). If Shopyos updates the text of any policy, the version number is incremented. When that happens, every user who tries to perform the relevant action is shown the new version and must agree again before proceeding. Old records are preserved so it is always possible to establish which version a user agreed to and when.

17. Open Issues for Legal Decision

The following items have not yet been resolved by legal counsel and require decisions before the platform goes live:

  1. Flash sale no-return clauses. The current policy states that flash sale items are not eligible for return. Counsel should confirm whether this is permissible under the Consumer Protection Agency Act, 2012 (Act 843) or whether a minimum return right must be preserved.
  2. Minimum return window. The platform currently defaults to a 7-day return window, which administrators can change. What is the minimum return window the platform is legally required to offer?
  3. Inter-regional parcel liability. The Inter-Regional Shipping Terms do not yet clearly state who bears responsibility for goods that are lost or damaged in transit between hubs. This should be explicitly set out in the disclaimer text.
  4. Driver liability for damaged goods. Similarly, the Driver Agreement does not address liability for goods damaged during local last-mile delivery. This should be resolved before drivers are onboarded at scale.
  5. Data retention period. How long must consent records and order data be retained? The current system retains records indefinitely. GDPR guidance suggests a minimum of 6 months for consent records; local law requirements should be confirmed.
  6. Bargain confidentiality. The bargaining terms should explicitly state that no buyer's offer is ever shared with other buyers or publicly visible — to avoid any claim of misleading auction-style pricing.
  7. Governing jurisdiction. The Terms of Service and Privacy Policy should explicitly state which Ghanaian laws govern disputes arising from platform use, and whether arbitration is available as an alternative to litigation.
